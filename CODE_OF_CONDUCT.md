# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, the maintainers and contributors of the **USTC Payment Portal**, pledge to foster an inclusive, safe, and respectful environment for everyone—users, contributors, and maintainers alike.

We are committed to making participation in this project a harassment‑free experience for all, regardless of:

- Age
- Body size
- Disability
- Ethnicity
- Sex characteristics
- Gender identity and expression
- Level of experience
- Nationality
- Personal appearance
- Race
- Religion (or lack thereof)
- Sexual orientation
- Socioeconomic status

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of **positive, constructive behavior** include:

- Demonstrating empathy toward others
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Taking responsibility and apologizing when necessary
- Focusing on what is best for users and the community
- Helping others learn and grow

Examples of **unacceptable behavior** include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination
- Insults, derogatory comments, or personal attacks
- Sexualized language or imagery
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others’ private information without permission
- Sustained disruption of discussions or collaboration

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing acceptable behavior, and they will take appropriate corrective action in response to any behavior deemed inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to:

- Remove or edit contributions not aligned with this Code of Conduct
- Temporarily or permanently ban contributors for repeated violations
- Moderate discussions and pull requests as needed

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project spaces, including GitHub Issues, PRs, Discussions, and documentation
- Public interactions involving community members, including social channels when representing the project

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will use the following guidelines when determining consequences for violations:

### 1. Correction
**Outcome:** A private, respectful reminder about conduct expectations.

### 2. Warning
**Outcome:** Formal warning with consequences for continued behavior.

### 3. Temporary Ban
**Outcome:** Temporary removal from project spaces for a set period.

### 4. Permanent Ban
**Outcome:** Permanent removal from project participation.
